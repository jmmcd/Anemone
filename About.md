# About Anemone

**Anemone** is an interactive evolutionary computation (IEC) library with a large collection of plug-in apps demonstrating what it can do.

**What is IEC?** You know how a farmer can breed sheep to become more woolly, over many generations, by always selecting the woolliest ones in the current generation to breed? IEC is just like that, and you are the farmer. Instead of sheep, we have:

* Drumbeats
* Melodies
* Cartoon bugs
* Robots
* Spidery creatures
* Photo filters
* Abstract pattern images
* Bioluminescent cells and seaweeds
* 3D sculptures
* And more!

**How to use it** Click the images you like best, then click "Evolve" (or the green circular arrow button, on mobile). You'll get a new generation of individuals which are created by combining the genes of the parents you selected, and then mutating them. Repeat that over and over until you get bored. Double-click to see a magnified version, and you'll also see a Save button. 

**Changing to a different app** When you do get bored, go to the menu, and you can choose a different app.

**Challenges** Do you prefer to just explore and see what happens, or do you find yourself searching for something particular? How hard is it to find something particular, and what are the best strategies? If you click "Evolve" without selecting anything, you get a selection of totally new individuals, unrelated to the previous generation. Is this useful or not?

**Extra details**

* The music apps can connect to a MIDI sequencer, if you have one! You might need to use Chrome (not Firefox or Safari), and you might need to enable WedMIDI. People who use MIDI sequencers probably know what they're doing here!

* Some of the apps require you to upload a photo for the app to work on. You'll see this in the menu.

* A couple of the apps allow you to directly edit, eg you can edit the drum track manually to fix a couple of wrong hits, and then continue evolving!

* Several of the apps also allow you edit the Javascript code which generates the individuals, and the code which visualises them. This is advanced usage, but don't hesitate to try it -- you can't break anything! Take a look at the code that pops up when you click "Edit code" in the menu and you'll find yourself learning some Javascript.
